The express entry program of Canada is offered to skilled professionals seeking citizenship.

The applicants are awarded points based on their skills and experiences. The applicants with higher points receive an Invitation to Apply (ITA).

The express entry program facilitates citizenship within 6-12 months, with the right skills and experiences.

What is the Canada Express entry program? Canada express entry program detail

The express entry program is an efficient immigration program, targeted at skilled professionals looking to migrate. The key details of the program include:

An online program, open throughout the year for the applicants.

The program applies only to Skilled Workers, traders, and Skilled Professionals

The applicants must submit an Expression of Interest and apply for any type of job mentioned in skill types 0, A, B.

Your profile will be evaluated and placed in the applicant pool based on points.

Canadian provinces and employers evaluate the pool of talents meeting their needs.

The highest point holders receive an invitation to apply for PR

The number of ITA’s issued depends on the annual immigration level.

Eligibility for Express Entry Programs

The Canada Express Entry Program is a point-based system to evaluate the applicants. The aim is to offer citizenship to applicants having the highest success rates after immigrating.

A point scale of 1200 is used to evaluate the applicants and other details including:

  • Age
  • Educational qualification
  • Language skills
  • Canadian work experience
  • Other work experience
  • Skill transferability
Benefits of the Express Entry System:

01

Express entry is a transparent program completely working on a point-based system. The candidates are required to score a specific CRS point to be eligible for an ITA of permanent residency.

02

The candidates are given certain eligibility points to be obtained in the CRS to qualify for an ITA. If the candidate does not qualify for the CRS points, they can try again or try other CRS options.

03

The candidates applying for the express entry program can also look at improving their language test results, gaining additional work experience, or even trying for a provincial nomination.

Better prospects for migrants:

The eligibility for the Express Entry program is based on the Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS). The candidates are evaluated for 1200 points based on various factors including the IELTS score and experience of studying or working in Canada. The candidate needs to score CRS based on the following factors:

  • Core human factor- 500 CRS
  • Skills transferability- 100 CRS
  • Additional factors- 600 CRS
  • Federal Skilled Workers Program (FSWP)
  • Federal Skilled Trades Program (FSTP)
  • Canadian Experience Class (CEC)

Proceeding with an ITA

After receiving an ITA, the candidates are required to complete an application form for further processing. The candidates are expected to apply within a 90-day time interval. The candidates failing to submit within the deadline will be considered null and void.

Submit an Application

Documents:

For the next step in applying after receiving an ITA, the candidates must provide the specified documents to apply for the PR visa- CEC or any other Express Entry Program. The required documents are mentioned in the Express Entry Portal. They include:

  • Documents supporting your English language test results
  • Civil status documents such as your birth certificate
  • Documents for educational qualification
  • Documents in proof of your work experience
  • Medical certificate
  • Police clearance certificate
  • Proof of funds
  • Photos

The candidates are required to provide a medical certificate from a doctor approved by the IRCC.

Based on the program applied the list of documents is subject to change. Ensure to prepare all the documents necessary.

Biometrics:

The applicants are required to give their biometrics. If the candidate has already given the biometric in the last ten years as a part of applying for a work permit, student visa, or visitor visa, they can be exempt from giving the biometrics again. It is to be noted that the exemption is a temporary move given the covid-19 pandemic.

The candidates can approach the nearest biometric collection centre to give their biometrics.

It is mandatory to complete the medical tests, and biometrics and have all the required documents before applying.
