Caregivers in canada

An expression of well-being for others. Explore the caregivers’ program with Connell.

Canada welcomes caregivers from around the globe. There are several opportunities to work as a caregiver and obtain a work permit or permanent residency in the process.

The process has been delayed recently due to the impact of covid-19. But the caregivers can apply for other temporary permits.

Covid-19 Effects on Programs

Due to issues of Covid-19, there are processing delays in offering permanent residencies or any form of work permit to caregivers in Canada.

The programs that were affected include:

  • Home Child Care Provider
  • Home Support Worker
  • Live-in Caregiver
  • Caring for Children
  • Caring for People with high Medical attention
  • Interim Pathway for Caregivers

Permanent Residence for Caregivers

Keeping your temporary resident status

The candidate must hold a temporary resident status while working in Canada. They must have:

  • Extend your work permit before it expires
  • Change the conditions before it expires

The candidates who applied for a new work permit before the current permit ends can continue working under the conditions of the old permit. This is called maintained status (previously called implied status).

Home Child Care Provider Pilot and Home Support Worker Pilot

You may be able to apply for permanent residence through the Home Child Care Provider Pilot or Home Support Worker Pilot if you:

  • meet the eligibility requirements, and
  • Have a job offer to work in one of these occupations

Through these pilots, the candidates are offered a work permit to temporarily work in Canada. The permit:

  • Is occupation restricted (restricted to the job)
  • Doesn’t require a Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA)
  • Let’s the candidate experience the work required for permanent residence

The candidate’s experience as a home childcare provider or a support worker is considered in the eligibility criteria for Permanent residency or citizenship.

Live-in Caregiver Program

The Live-in Caregiver program is currently closed for new applicants.

The candidates applying for permanent residency through the Live-in Caregiver program should have a minimum of 2 years of experience and:

  • Already working in Canada with an LCP work permit
  • Approved for a first LCP work permit on a Labour Market Impact Assessment submitted to Employment and Social Development Canada.

Candidates having work experience and not qualifying for any of the given programs can try immigration through different programs.

Temporary Work for Caregivers

Apply to extend your work permit

Candidates currently working as a caregiver in Canada can extend their work permits through the Temporary Foreign Worker Program (TFWP).

The employer must get a positive Labour market Impact Assessment first.

Apply for a new work permit

The candidates can apply for a temporary work permit in Canada through TFWP:

Candidates working in Canada and having eligibility to apply for a work permit can apply through this program. The employer must provide a positive LMIA for applying.

Applications we no longer process

The Caregiver program is temporarily not available under the following criteria:

Candidates applying from outside Canada Candidates applying through TFWP The employer offered for LMIA before June 18 of 2019.

Closed Programs

Caring for Children Program

The caregiver for children was closed as of June 18th, 2019. The applicants before June 18 are eligible for the caregiver program.

Candidates working as child caregivers in Canada are applicable for Home Childcare Provider Pilot.

Caring for people with high medical needs program

Caring for people with high medical needs ended by June 18th, 2019. The applications submitted before June 18th are eligible for processing.

Candidates working as home support workers are eligible for the Home Support Worker Pilot.

Interim Pathway for caregivers

The interim pathway for caregivers ended on October 8th, 2019. Candidates who have submitted their application before October 8th are eligible for processing.

Candidates not eligible under any program can apply for other immigration visa processes.
