Why study in Canada ?

Canada offers a global market of job opportunities, and many people apply to work there. The express entry program is specialized for skilled professionals looking to work in a different city.

Canada offers the best education recognized globally. The universities offer research facilities as a key component.

The students are encouraged to come up with new research ideas and scholarships are provided according to the research work.

More reasons to choose canada:

Numerous research opportunities – Universities support the entire research activities of students with innovative research ideas.

Possibility of immigration after completing the course –Candidates are awarded points based on categories like work, educational experience, and language proficiency. Additional points are awarded for candidates meeting specific age criteria. The points help the candidate with entering programs like Express entry or Provincial Nominee Streams.

Vibrant campus atmosphere -The universities offer a vibrant environment for the students. It encourages them to interact with the diverse multi-cultural community.

Opportunity to work while studying - Canada offers a Post Graduate Work Permit Program (PGWP), which allows students to access a work permit for up to three years after graduation.

Internship opportunities -The universities offer internship programs to students studying and graduating from Canada.

Enhance your exposure through learning, prepare yourself for a global market.

Eligibility Criteria :

Educational Qualification

The candidates must have minimum eligibility criteria of about 50% in UGC or AICTE recognized university.

Year Back

The candidate must complete the respective course in the given time without a gap year. Example: A B.Sc. is a three-year course and must be completed within the time frame.

Backlogs

The candidate should not have more than 10 backlogs during the graduation period.

Stay back period in canada and Post Graduation work permit :

Candidates willing to stay back for post-graduation must enrol in a course for a minimum of 8 months.

Courses less than 8 months duration are not eligible for a stay back or work permit.

For post-graduation work permit a student needs to pursue a course of:

For courses less than 2 years duration, a work permit for the same duration of the course is offered. E.g., 8 months course= 8-month work permit

Students pursuing a 2-year course have eligibility for a three-year post-graduate work permit.

Cost of Studying in Canada

For international students, the yearly fees and tuition fees range from $7000 to 35000 based on the course selected.

The tuition fees vary for different institutions and provinces. But the overall academic fees are more affordable compared to other countries. Technical and medical courses are expensive compared to art courses.

Upcoming Intakes in the Canada

Canada universities and colleges offer three intakes. They are also be referred as semesters in some institutions. The three intakes available in Canada are:

Intake 1: Fall Semester – A popular intake starts in September

Intake 2: Winter Semester – starts in January

Intake 3: Summer Semester – Starts in April/May (available only for limited programs and colleges).

Candidates are requested to apply well in advance. It is difficult to get your college of choice closer to the deadline. The candidates should apply 6-9 months in advance.

Work Authorization of Students

Student Applicant:

The student must be 18 years of age.

Students studying in Canada are eligible to work for 20 hours per week during the university semester, full-time or part-time, and during the summer breaks and winter breaks.

Foreign students who need work experiences such as through a co-op or internship program.

Spouse:

The spouse of the candidate experiences the same rights as the candidate. The spouse of the candidate can work in Canada until the candidate’s study permit is valid. However, if the spouse chooses to study, they are required to apply for a study permit individually.

Steps to study abroad in Canada:

01.

Research Your Options

Conduct an overall analysis of the course, universities offering the course, cost, accommodation, and other details before shortlisting the university.

02.

Select universities

Shortlist about 10 universities based on your course offering and choose the university that meets your requirements.

03.

Prepare for entrance exams

Check for the eligibility criteria for the universities and prepare for the IELTS, TOEFL, GRE, and GMAT accordingly.

04.

05.

07.

Get a student visa

Apply for a student’s visa once the application is confirmed.

Student Visa and Dependents

Get the best guidance from our expert counsellors and prepare ahead.

Students applying for a study permit can bring their spouses and children with them. The spouse is eligible to work under a Canadian employer under the study permit of their partner is valid.

However, to study in Canada the spouse has to apply for a separate study permit.

The candidates with a study permit can bring their children with them to Canada. The children can stay with the candidate in Canada. Minor children of the candidates are applicable to pursue education at the schools in Canada until the validity of the candidate’s study permit.

A dependent child must be below 22 years. Children above 22, who are not financially independent due to a physical or mental disability are considered under dependents.

After you Graduate

Canada offers programs for students studying and graduating in Canada with work experience and an opportunity to contribute to Canada’s culture and economy.

Canada offers a Post Graduate Work Permit Program, which allows students to access a work permit for up to three years after graduation.

The candidates can apply to work through this program without any job offer from any Canadian employer.

PR Visa options

Candidates studying in abroad gain points in areas such as education, and work experience, and additional points are offered for candidates meeting certain age criteria. Educational experience in Canada offers various points to apply through other streams like the Express Entry stream or Provincial Nominee Stream.

Students studying in Canada are eligible to work for 20 hours per week, which helps them with the CRS score as a part of their work experience.

Studying in Canada offers a cultural diversity with the place, people, and language proficiency (English or French), which will help during the permanent residency.
